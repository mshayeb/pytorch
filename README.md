## Fundamentals

- **PyTorch "60-Minute Blitz"** (official tutorial). A hands-on sprint through tensors, autograd, and training a tiny neural net entirely in PyTorch. [docs.pytorch.org](https://docs.pytorch.org/tutorials/beginner/deep_learning_60min_blitz.html)

- **3Blue1Brown: Neural Networks (Ch. 1–2).** Visual, intuition-first walkthrough of neurons, weights, and gradient descent/backprop basics. [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi)

- **Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville — Deep Learning (Ch. 1–6).** A rigorous foundation covering core math, optimization, and the basic anatomy of deep networks. [deeplearningbook.org](https://www.deeplearningbook.org/)

## AI: History, Background & Fundamentals

- **Melanie Mitchell — Artificial Intelligence: A Guide for Thinking Humans.** An accessible, balanced tour of what AI can and can't do and why that matters. [threetreesbooks.com](https://threetreesbooks.com/book/9781250758040)

- **Nils J. Nilsson — The Quest for Artificial Intelligence** (free PDF). A comprehensive historical narrative of AI from early symbolic systems to modern machine learning. [ai.stanford.edu](https://ai.stanford.edu/~nilsson/QAI/qai.pdf)

## General Machine Learning

- **Bishop — Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning (PRML).** The gold-standard mathematical treatment of probabilistic models and learning algorithms.

- **Aurélien Géron — Hands-On Machine Learning.** A practical, project-driven guide to building, evaluating, and shipping ML systems.

- **DeepLearning.AI — Machine Learning Specialization** (Week 1 especially). A clear conceptual intro to supervised learning, key metrics, and the ML workflow. [Deeplearning.ai](https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/machine-learning-specialization)

## PyTorch (Philosophy, Ecosystem, Getting Started)

- **What is PyTorch / Docs Hub.** Overview of PyTorch's eager-execution philosophy, core APIs, and ecosystem libraries. [docs.pytorch.org](https://docs.pytorch.org/)

- **PyTorch 2.x quick look** (compile, performance). A tour of `torch.compile`/Inductor and the performance upgrades in the 2.x release line. [PyTorch 2.x](https://pytorch.org/get-started/pytorch-2-x/)

- **Book: Deep Learning with PyTorch** (Manning, 2e). End-to-end patterns and recipes for building, training, and deploying PyTorch models. [Manning Publications](https://www.manning.com/books/deep-learning-with-pytorch-second-edition)

- **fast.ai book/course.** A top-down, PyTorch-first course that gets you training strong models quickly with real projects. [Practical Deep Learning for Coders](https://course.fast.ai/Resources/book.html)

## Deep Learning: General & Theory

- **Goodfellow/Bengio/Courville.** The definitive textbook on DL theory, covering optimization, regularization, CNNs, and more. [deeplearningbook.org](https://www.deeplearningbook.org/)

- **Michael Nielsen — Neural Networks and Deep Learning** (free online). A gentle, interactive explanation of backpropagation and core DL ideas. [Neural Networks and Deep Learning](http://neuralnetworksanddeeplearning.com/)

- **CS231n Notes: Neural Networks Part 1.** Practical guidance on activations, initialization, regularization, and training best practices. [cs231n.github.io](https://cs231n.github.io/)

- **DeepLearning.AI — Deep Learning Specialization.** A foundational program covering neural networks, CNNs, RNNs, optimization techniques, and practical deep learning applications. [Deeplearning.ai](https://www.deeplearning.ai/courses/deep-learning-specialization/)

## ETL & the ML Lifecycle

- **Google: Rules of Machine Learning.** Best practices for designing, iterating, and maintaining production ML systems. [martin.zinkevich.org](https://martin.zinkevich.org/rules_of_ml/rules_of_ml.pdf)

- **Google Cloud — MLOps: Continuous delivery & automation pipelines (CI/CD/CT).** Patterns for CI/CD/CT, model versioning, and reliable deployment/monitoring. [Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/architecture/mlops-continuous-delivery-and-automation-pipelines-in-machine-learning)

## Neural Networks

- **Welch Labs.** Friendly videos plus an interactive tool to build intuition for layers, activations, and decision boundaries. [Neural Networks Demystified](https://www.welchlabs.com/blog/2015/1/16/neural-networks-demystified-part-1-data-and-architecture)

- **CS231n "Neural Networks Part 1".** Clear notes on MLPs, loss functions, and practical training heuristics. [cs231n.github.io](https://cs231n.github.io/)

- **MIT 6.S191 slides** (optional visual companion). A concise slide deck covering deep-learning fundamentals with helpful visuals and pointers. [cs231n.stanford.edu](https://cs231n.stanford.edu/slides/2017/cs231n_2017_lecture6.pdf)

- **CME 296 Diffusion & Large Vision Models. This course explores diffusion-based generative models for vision. You will study the foundations of diffusion, score matching and flow matching, modern architectures such as U-Nets and Diffusion Transformers, and methods for controllable image generation and evaluation. [cme296](https://cme296.stanford.edu/)
